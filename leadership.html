1.     Air Vice Marshal Mohammed Adamu Idris hails from Maiduguri Local Government of Borno State. He attended Army Children School and Boys Secondary School Gindiri Plateau State before proceeding to the Nigerian Defence Academy for training as a Regular Combatant officer cadet. He was commissioned into the Nigerian Air Force as a Pilot Officer on 19 Dec 87 and commenced ab-initio flying training at the then 301 Flying Training School NAF Base Kaduna. He later proceeded for basic and tactical combat flying training at the then 303 Flying Training School NAF Base Kano and 4th College China respectively. Following successful completion of both flying training courses, in 2006 the Commandant underwent Alpha Jet Tactical Combat Training at the 99 Air Weapons School Kainji. As a combat-rated fighter pilot, the Commandant further underwent Air Combat Training for the both Chinese and Nigerian variants of the supersonic F-7Ni fighter aircraft at Changzhou China and at the 64 Air Defence Group NAF Base Makurdi.    Read More...
